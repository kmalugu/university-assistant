UNIVERSITY ACADEMIC POLICIES AND STUDENT HANDBOOK
==================================================

SECTION 1: ATTENDANCE POLICY
------------------------------
1.1 Minimum attendance of 75% is mandatory in each course.
1.2 Students falling below 75% will be debarred from end-semester exams.
1.3 Medical leave requires submission of a doctor's certificate within 3 working days.
1.4 Students on official duty (NCC, NSS, Sports) are marked present if prior permission is obtained.
1.5 Condonation of shortage in attendance (up to 10%) may be considered by the HOD in genuine cases with written application.

SECTION 2: GRADING POLICY
--------------------------
2.1 The university follows a 10-point CGPA system.
2.2 Grade points: O (Outstanding) = 10, A+ = 9, A = 8, B+ = 7, B = 6, C = 5, P (Pass) = 4, F (Fail) = 0.
2.3 Continuous Internal Assessment (CIA) carries 40% and End Semester Exam (ESE) carries 60% weightage.
2.4 Internal marks: IA-1 (15) + IA-2 (15) + Assignments (10) = 40 marks.
2.5 Minimum 40% required separately in CIA and ESE to pass a course.
2.6 Grade moderation may be applied by the examinations board.
2.7 Results are declared within 30 days of the last exam.

SECTION 3: EXAMINATION RULES
------------------------------
3.1 Students must carry their Hall Ticket and ID card to all exams.
3.2 Electronic devices including mobile phones are strictly prohibited in exam halls.
3.3 Malpractice will lead to cancellation of the examination and disciplinary action.
3.4 Supplementary exams are conducted for students who fail in up to 2 subjects.
3.5 A re-totaling request must be submitted within 15 days of result declaration (fee: Rs. 200/subject).
3.6 A re-examination fee of Rs. 1500/subject applies for supplementary exams.

SECTION 4: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY
------------------------------
4.1 Plagiarism in assignments, projects, or thesis is a serious academic offence.
4.2 Any copied work will receive zero marks; repeat offenders face suspension.
4.3 Proper citation of references is mandatory in all academic submissions.
4.4 The university uses Turnitin for plagiarism detection. Similarity index must be below 20%.

SECTION 5: COURSE REGISTRATION
--------------------------------
5.1 Students must register for courses each semester within the prescribed window.
5.2 Late registration is permitted with a fine of Rs. 500.
5.3 Course add/drop is allowed within the first two weeks of the semester without academic penalty.
5.4 Dropping a course after two weeks will be reflected as 'W' (Withdrawn) in the transcript.
5.5 Students must register for a minimum of 15 credits and a maximum of 25 credits per semester.
5.6 CGPA requirements for lateral entry into advanced courses must be maintained.

SECTION 6: HOSTEL RULES
------------------------
6.1 All hostels have separate blocks for male and female students.
6.2 Hostel allotment is done on a first-come-first-served basis (priority to outstation students).
6.3 Visitors are allowed in the common room from 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM only.
6.4 Curfew time: 10:00 PM on weekdays, 11:00 PM on weekends.
6.5 Cooking in rooms is strictly prohibited. Mess facilities are available.
6.6 Students must vacate hostel during summer vacation (except with special permission).
6.7 Hostel fee must be paid by the 15th of the respective month.
6.8 Any damage to hostel property will be recovered from the student's caution deposit.

SECTION 7: LIBRARY RULES
-------------------------
7.1 Library hours: 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM (Mon-Fri), 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Sat-Sun).
7.2 Students can borrow up to 4 books (BTech/MSc), 5 books (MBA), or 6 books (PhD) at a time.
7.3 Loan period: 14 days for regular books, 3 days for reference books.
7.4 Late return fine: Rs. 2/day per book.
7.5 Mobile phones should be in silent mode inside the library.
7.6 E-resources (journals, e-books) are accessible via the university VPN.
7.7 Damaged or lost books must be replaced or compensated at current market price plus 10%.

SECTION 8: LAB SAFETY RULES
-----------------------------
8.1 Students must wear appropriate safety equipment (goggles, gloves, lab coat) in all labs.
8.2 Food and drink are strictly prohibited in all labs.
8.3 Students must log their presence in the lab register.
8.4 Any breakage or damage to lab equipment must be reported immediately to the lab instructor.
8.5 Computers in labs should not be used for personal browsing or gaming.
8.6 All USB drives must be scanned for viruses before use on lab computers.
8.7 Emergency exits must be kept clear at all times.

SECTION 9: PLACEMENT GUIDELINES
----------------------------------
9.1 All eligible students must register on the Placement Portal by the end of the 6th semester (BTech) or 2nd semester (MBA).
9.2 Students may apply to companies matching their eligibility criteria.
9.3 Once a student accepts a job offer, they are considered placed and cannot apply further (one-company policy in first round).
9.4 Students with active backlogs are not eligible for placement drives.
9.5 Resume must be approved by the placement cell before submission.
9.6 Pre-placement training includes Aptitude, Technical, and HR rounds — attendance is mandatory.
9.7 CGPA cutoff for most companies: >= 6.0 (some require >= 7.5).
9.8 Internship offers will be counted as placements if converted to PPO (Pre-Placement Offer).

SECTION 10: INTERNATIONAL STUDENT GUIDELINES
----------------------------------------------
10.1 International students must carry their passport and valid student visa at all times on campus.
10.2 Visa renewal assistance is provided by the International Student Cell (ISC), Admin Block, Room 101.
10.3 FRRO (Foreigners Regional Registration Office) registration must be completed within 14 days of arrival.
10.4 English language support classes are available for students who require them.
10.5 International students are eligible for on-campus hostel accommodation with priority.
10.6 Health insurance is mandatory for all international students — the university provides a group policy.
10.7 The ISC conducts orientation programs at the beginning of each academic year.
10.8 Fee payment may be made in foreign currency through SWIFT transfer (contact Finance Office).

SECTION 11: STUDENT CODE OF CONDUCT
--------------------------------------
11.1 Ragging in any form is strictly prohibited and punishable by expulsion.
11.2 Harassment (physical, verbal, or online) will lead to disciplinary action.
11.3 Students must wear their ID cards visibly at all times on campus.
11.4 Smoking and consumption of alcohol on campus is strictly prohibited.
11.5 Political activity, demonstrations, or unauthorized gatherings are not permitted without prior approval.
11.6 Any grievances must be reported to the Student Grievance Cell.

SECTION 12: MEDICAL AND COUNSELING SERVICES
---------------------------------------------
12.1 The University Health Center (UHC) is open 24x7 for emergency services.
12.2 OPD hours: 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM, Monday to Saturday.
12.3 Free consultation and basic medicines are provided to all enrolled students.
12.4 The Counseling Center offers confidential mental health support.
12.5 Counseling sessions can be booked at counseling@university.edu or call extension 6600.
12.6 Students requiring hospital referral will be facilitated by UHC staff.

SECTION 13: STUDENT CLUBS AND ACTIVITIES
------------------------------------------
13.1 Students are encouraged to join/form clubs related to academics, arts, sports, and social causes.
13.2 Club activities must be registered with the Student Activity Council (SAC).
13.3 Clubs may apply for funding from SAC for events and activities.
13.4 Participation in extracurricular activities is reflected in the Activity Points system.
13.5 A minimum of 30 activity points is required for degree completion (BTech/MSc).
